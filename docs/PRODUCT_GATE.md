# Product Gate

## Status

**NOT PASSED**

Product implementation must not begin until the gate is explicitly changed to `PASSED` by the Product Owner after manager review.

## Purpose

The gate prevents building a visually impressive but operationally false system that cannot calculate prices, route orders, promise realistic stages or represent the actual manufacturer workflow.

## Required business evidence

### Manufacturer and product data

- [ ] Product catalogue or representative assortment.
- [ ] Materials, colors, sizes and available processing options.
- [ ] Wholesale/production prices or pricing formulas.
- [ ] Retail pricing policy and permitted discount rules.
- [ ] Standard and custom production capabilities.
- [ ] Realistic production stages and dependencies.
- [ ] Seasonal capacity and lead-time constraints.
- [ ] Defect, remake and warranty procedures.
- [ ] Rules for providing showroom samples.

### Installation and field operations

- [ ] Installation geography.
- [ ] Installation price rules.
- [ ] Delivery and unloading rules.
- [ ] Site survey checklist.
- [ ] Required cemetery documents/notifications.
- [ ] Typical restrictions by cemetery or installation type.
- [ ] Foundation and soil decision rules.
- [ ] Photo evidence and acceptance process.
- [ ] Weather and seasonal restrictions.

### Sales process

- [ ] Current lead sources.
- [ ] Real customer questions and objections.
- [ ] Current sales stages.
- [ ] Existing quote/estimate examples.
- [ ] Existing contract and warranty documents.
- [ ] Payment schedule and refund/change rules.
- [ ] Manager responsibilities and response standards.
- [ ] Reasons for won and lost orders, if available.

### Legal and operational identity

- [ ] Selling legal entity or approved interim model.
- [ ] Who signs the customer contract.
- [ ] Who is responsible for manufacturing, delivery and installation.
- [ ] Personal-data operator and hosting constraints.
- [ ] Consent texts and document retention policy.
- [ ] Payment provider requirements, if online payment is planned.
- [ ] Brand name and permitted claims.

### Media and evidence plan

Real media may be added later, but before public launch the following must be defined:

- [ ] list of required showroom photographs;
- [ ] production photo/video shot list;
- [ ] installed-project photo standard;
- [ ] permission to publish each project;
- [ ] rules for before/after content;
- [ ] team and responsible-person content;
- [ ] proof of materials and quality-control content.

## Required user research

At minimum, collect structured evidence from:

- [ ] 5 recent buyers or family decision-makers;
- [ ] 2 salespeople or people who regularly consult buyers;
- [ ] manufacturer representative;
- [ ] designer/engraving specialist;
- [ ] installer or field specialist.

Interview topics:

- what was hardest to understand;
- what information was missing;
- how the budget was formed;
- what caused distrust;
- where mistakes or repeated communication occurred;
- what information clients repeatedly requested;
- what delayed approval, production or installation;
- what should never be fully automated.

## Required product decisions

- [ ] Primary launch audience and geography.
- [ ] First buyer modes supported.
- [ ] MVP boundary.
- [ ] What price can be shown publicly.
- [ ] What constitutes a qualified case.
- [ ] Required human review points.
- [ ] Customer-visible status model.
- [ ] First version of the domain model.
- [ ] Success metrics and baseline collection.
- [ ] Explicit list of deferred capabilities.

## Gate review package

Before the gate can pass, the Project Manager prepares:

1. validated customer journey;
2. service blueprint across customer, sales, design, production and installation;
3. pricing confidence model;
4. MVP capability map;
5. prioritized risk register;
6. revised technical specification;
7. first implementation roadmap with vertical slices;
8. initial architecture decision record;
9. measurable acceptance criteria for Release 1.

## Gate decision record

When ready, add:

- decision date;
- decision owner;
- evidence reviewed;
- remaining accepted risks;
- approved Release 1 boundary;
- link to implementation epic/issue.
